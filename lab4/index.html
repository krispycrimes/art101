Lab 4 - Pseudocoding and Problem-solving

Challenge

Get in the habit of thinking in terms of pseudocoding before jumping into coding.

Problems

Our challenges were figuring out how much we needed to break everything down to steps in order to communicate and define fully the task at hand. We were wondering at what level of understanding and assumptions can be made while defining a task and its steps but we assumed that we had to explain it to a robot or an alien who would follow our instructions literally. Minesweeper was an interesting problem as I had vague memories of it and so my lack of familiarity helped with defining the different steps that had to be defined as I find sometimes when something is intuitive to us, we can overlook assumptions we make in the process that isn't immediately obvious to those unfamiliar. On the other hand, because of my lack of familiarity with the game, understanding the mechanics was something I had to learn along the way.

Reflection

For this assignment, we worked together to create a list of tasks for baking cookies and for implementing the game Minesweeper. We then put that pseudocode as comments into our javascript file. We faced some challenges trying to break down baking into as simple steps as possible. We started with the basic set of instructions for baking, then continued to break it down into even smaller and simpler steps, imagining we were trying to teach someone who had never baked before. It was also quite difficult figuring out how to approach pseudocoding minesweeper. Although it seemed like a simple game, we quickly realized a lot of the complexities and definitions that had to be established before we could even start the game. We turned to some online resources to understand how Minesweeper usually works, then translated that into pseudo code.
